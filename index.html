Many of us have likely had the experience of chatting on the telephone with a busy friend who during the conversation is scanning to-do lists, opening e-mail, and the like.

Despite his or her best efforts to seem engaged in the interaction, we can nonetheless often detect that person's multitasking behavior and use that perception as a cue to let our rushed and distracted friend go.

even in the absence of visual cues and detailed contextual information, sensitive listeners can frequently make accurate and important inferences about the emotional and cognitive states of others from the way they are speaking with us, rather than from just the content of what they are saying.
